<p><p>Fiber in the Loop refers to the deployment of fiber optic cables to provide high-speed internet connectivity. In the Fiber Laid to the Premise market, the fiber cables are extended directly to individual premises, such as homes and businesses, ensuring reliable and fast internet access. On the other hand, in the Fiber Laid to the Node market, the fiber cables are laid only up to a central point (node) in the neighborhood, and then traditional copper cables are used for the last mile connection to individual premises, which may result in slightly slower speeds.</p></p>
