<p><p>Biomass Utilization systems offer various methods for converting organic waste into energy. Direct combustion involves burning biomass to produce heat or electricity. Anaerobic digestion breaks down organic materials in a controlled environment to produce biogas. Fermentation is a process that converts biomass into biofuels, such as ethanol. Oil extraction involves extracting oils from biomass for various applications. Pyrolysis breaks down biomass at high temperatures without oxygen. Gasification converts biomass into combustible gases for energy production. Each method offers unique benefits for biomass utilization.</p></p>
