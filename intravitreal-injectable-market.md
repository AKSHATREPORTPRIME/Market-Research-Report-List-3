<p><p>Intravitreal injectables are drugs that are administered directly into the eye's vitreous (the gel-like substance that fills the eye), bypassing the need for oral or systemic administration. These injectables are primarily used in the treatment of various ocular disorders such as age-related macular degeneration (AMD), diabetic retinopathy, macular edema, and retinal vein occlusion.</p><p>The global intravitreal injectable market is projected to witness steady growth during the forecast period. Factors contributing to this growth include the increasing prevalence of eye disorders, growing geriatric population, rising adoption of minimally invasive treatments, and advancements in drug delivery technologies. Moreover, the high success rate of intravitreal injectables in treating retinal diseases and the growing demand for improved vision care services also drive the market growth.</p><p>Additionally, the market is witnessing several recent trends. One key trend is the development and adoption of long-acting intravitreal injectable formulations. These formulations help reduce the number of injections required by patients and provide prolonged therapeutic effects. Moreover, the emergence of gene therapy-based intravitreal injectables is gaining traction in the market. Gene therapy offers the potential for targeted treatment by correcting the underlying genetic defects responsible for certain eye diseases.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is also witnessing advancements in drug delivery devices, such as self-administered injectable devices, which enhance patient convenience and compliance. <p><p>Intravitreal injectable market includes two major types - Anti-VEGF and Steroids. Anti-VEGF (anti-vascular endothelial growth factor) drugs are commonly used for treating retinal diseases, such as age-related macular degeneration and diabetic retinopathy. They work by inhibiting the growth of abnormal blood vessels in the eye. Steroids, on the other hand, are used to treat various eye conditions, including inflammation and swelling. They work by reducing inflammation and suppressing the immune response. Both types of drugs are administered directly into the vitreous cavity of the eye through injections.</p></p>
