<p><p>Friction modifiers are additives used in lubricants to reduce friction and improve the efficiency of mechanical systems. Organic friction modifiers are derived from natural sources such as fatty acids and esters, while inorganic friction modifiers are typically metallic compounds like molybdenum disulfide and tungsten disulfide. Organic friction modifiers are known for their environmental friendliness and biodegradability, while inorganic friction modifiers are valued for their high performance and durability. Both types offer unique benefits and are used in various industries depending on specific lubrication needs.</p></p>
