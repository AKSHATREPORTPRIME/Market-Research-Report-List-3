<p><p>Skin substitutes are innovative products used for the treatment of skin wounds or ulcers. Acellular substitutes, obtained from human or animal sources, lack living cells and provide a scaffold for tissue regeneration. Cellular allogeneic substitutes, derived from donated human cells, contain living cells and promote healing by releasing growth factors. Cellular autologous substitutes utilize the patient's own cells to stimulate tissue regeneration. Other types may include synthetic substitutes or combination products. Each type offers unique advantages and is designed to accelerate wound healing and improve the quality of healed skin.</p></p>
