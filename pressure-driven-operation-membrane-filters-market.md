<p><p>Pressure driven operation membrane filters operate using pressure to push a liquid through a porous membrane, separating particles based on their size. The market types include microfiltration, which filters particles of 0.1-10 microns; ultrafiltration, for particles of 0.001-0.1 microns; nanofiltration, for particles between 0.001-0.01 microns; and reverse osmosis, which filters particles smaller than 0.001 microns. Each type of membrane filter is designed for specific applications based on the size of particles being filtered.</p></p>
