<p><p>Biocatalysis involves the use of biological catalysts to accelerate chemical reactions. Biocatalysts can be categorized into different types based on their specific functions, such as hydrolases, oxidoreductases, transferases, and others. Hydrolases catalyze the breakdown of molecules through hydrolysis, oxidoreductases facilitate oxidation-reduction reactions, and transferases transfer functional groups between molecules. The "others" category includes various enzymes with different catalytic activities. Each type of biocatalyst plays a unique role in enhancing reaction efficiency and selectivity in industrial processes.</p></p>
