<p><p>Neutral Density (ND) Filters are categorized into three types based on their density levels. ND filters less than 0.3 are ideal for reducing light in slightly bright conditions. ND filters ranging from 0.3 to 3.0 are suitable for moderately bright to very bright environments. ND filters with a density level greater than 3.0 are perfect for extremely bright conditions, such as shooting in broad daylight or capturing long exposures during daylight hours. Each type of ND filter serves a specific purpose in photography and videography.</p></p>
