<p><p>Geotechnical cloth is available in two main types: synthetic and natural. Synthetic geotechnical cloth is made from materials like polyester, polypropylene, and polyethylene, offering high strength, durability, and resistance to environmental conditions. Natural geotechnical cloth, on the other hand, is typically made from materials like jute, coir, and flax, providing a more sustainable and eco-friendly option. Both synthetic and natural geotechnical cloths are used in various engineering applications such as erosion control, soil stabilization, and drainage systems.</p></p>
