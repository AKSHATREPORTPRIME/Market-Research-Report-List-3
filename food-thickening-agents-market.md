<p><p>Food thickening agents are essential in the food industry to improve texture, viscosity, and stability of various food products. The market is mainly classified into three types: Hydrocolloids, Protein, and Starch. Hydrocolloids, such as agar and carrageenan, are derived from natural sources and have unique thickening properties. Proteins, like gelatin and egg white, are commonly used for thickening and stabilizing food products. Starches, such as cornstarch and tapioca, are widely utilized for thickening sauces, soups, and gravies. Each type of thickening agent offers different functionalities based on the specific requirements of the food product.</p></p>
