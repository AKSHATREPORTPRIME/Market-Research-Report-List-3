<p><p>Market pulp refers to wood pulp produced for sale on the open market, rather than for internal consumption by a paper or tissue manufacturer. It is categorized into different grades based on the type of wood used and the manufacturing process. BSK (Bleached Softwood Kraft) pulp is made from softwood trees and bleached to achieve a high brightness level. BHK (Bleached Hardwood Kraft) pulp is produced from hardwood trees and also bleached. HYP (High Yield Pulp) is made using mechanical or chemi-mechanical pulping processes, resulting in a higher yield of pulp from the raw material. These different market types cater to various end-uses in paper and tissue production.</p></p>
