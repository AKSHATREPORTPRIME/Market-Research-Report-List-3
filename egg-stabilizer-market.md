<p><p>Egg stabilizers are commonly used in baking to improve the texture and stability of baked goods. The market for egg stabilizers can be categorized into three main types: cream of tartar, sugar, and starch. Cream of tartar is a natural acid that helps stabilize egg whites, while sugar can help add structure and sweetness to baked goods. Starch, such as cornstarch or arrowroot, can also help thicken and stabilize batters and doughs. Each type of egg stabilizer serves a different purpose in baking, catering to different needs and preferences.</p></p>
