<p><p>The Radio Frequency (RF) Cable market includes two main types of cables, Coaxial Cables and Fiber-Optic Cables. Coaxial cables consist of a copper core surrounded by insulation and a conductive shield, allowing them to transmit radio frequencies with low signal loss. Fiber-optic cables, on the other hand, use light pulses to transmit data through thin strands of glass or plastic. Each type of cable has unique advantages and is used in various applications within the RF industry.</p></p>
