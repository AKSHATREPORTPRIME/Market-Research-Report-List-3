<p><p>Vaccine delivery devices refer to tools used for administering vaccines through specific routes. Intranasal delivery devices are designed to deliver vaccines to the nasal cavity, where they are absorbed through the nasal mucosa. Intradermal delivery devices, on the other hand, are used to deliver vaccines directly into the skin, typically into the dermis layer. These devices ensure effective vaccine absorption and offer advantages like painless administration, reduced needle waste, and ease of use. The development and utilization of such devices contribute to efficient and convenient vaccine delivery.</p></p>
