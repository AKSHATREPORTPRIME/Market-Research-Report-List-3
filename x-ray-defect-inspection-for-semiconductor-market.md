<p><ul><li>X-ray Diffraction Imaging (XRDI)</li><li>Broadband Plasma Patterned</li><li>e-Beam Patterned</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>X-Ray Defect Inspection is a technique used in the semiconductor market to identify defects in semiconductor materials. X-ray Diffraction Imaging (XRDI) utilizes X-ray beams to analyze the atomic structure of a sample, providing detailed information about its defects. Broadband Plasma Patterned inspection uses plasma to generate high-energy X-rays, which detect defects by analyzing the sample's conductivity. e-Beam Patterned inspection employs an electron beam to create an X-ray image, enabling defect identification. Other techniques, such as acoustic microscopy and thermal wave imaging, are also used for defect inspection in the semiconductor market.</p></p>
