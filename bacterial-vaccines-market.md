<p><p>Bacterial vaccines refer to medical products that target specific bacteria to prevent infections. Capsular polysaccharide vaccines consist of the bacteria's outer coating, while toxoid vaccines utilize inactivated toxins produced by bacteria. Purified protein vaccines use specific bacterial proteins to stimulate immunity. Live attenuated bacterial vaccines contain weakened bacteria, while killed bacterial vaccines use inactivated bacteria. Other types may include recombinant vaccines or DNA-based vaccines. These different approaches serve to elicit an immune response in the body and protect individuals from bacterial infections.</p></p>
