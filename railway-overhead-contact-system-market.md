<p><p>A Railway Overhead Contact System is a power supply system used by electric railways to transfer power from overhead lines to trains. This system consists of overhead wires called catenaries that supply electricity to trains through a pantograph mechanism attached to the top of the train carriage. <p><ul><li>Simple Catenary</li><li>Stitched Catenary</li><li>Compound Catenary</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Railway Overhead Contact System Market Types refer to different types of structures used to supply electricity to trains. Simple Catenary consists of a single wire suspended above the tracks. Stitched Catenary has additional wires for extra support and stability. Compound Catenary uses a combination of wires and cables to provide power distribution. Each type of system has its own advantages and is chosen based on factors such as speed requirements, train weight, and track layout. These systems play a crucial role in ensuring efficient and safe operation of railway networks.</p></p>
