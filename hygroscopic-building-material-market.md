<p><p>Hygroscopic building materials can be categorized into different types. Inorganic minerals/clays such as calcium chloride and lithium chloride have hygroscopic properties that allow them to absorb moisture from the air. Inorganic salts like magnesium nitrate are also commonly used for their hygroscopic abilities. Natural hygroscopic materials like wood and straw can absorb and release moisture as needed. Molecular sieves and silica/silicon dioxide are known for their high absorbency rates. Other materials, such as certain polymers, are also utilized for their hygroscopic properties in the building industry.</p></p>
