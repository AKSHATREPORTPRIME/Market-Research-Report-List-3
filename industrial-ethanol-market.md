<p><p>Industrial Ethanol is primarily categorized into Agriculture Ethanol and Synthetic Ethanol markets. Agriculture Ethanol is produced from agricultural feedstocks such as corn, sugarcane, and wheat, while Synthetic Ethanol is derived from chemical processes using feedstocks like coal, natural gas, and biomass. Agriculture Ethanol is commonly used as a biofuel additive in gasoline, whereas Synthetic Ethanol is typically used in industrial applications such as solvents, disinfectants, and as a precursor in chemical synthesis.
