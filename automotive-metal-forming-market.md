<p><p>Automotive metal forming involves various techniques such as roll forming, stretch forming, stamping, deep drawing, hydroforming, and others. Roll forming is used to produce long pieces of metal with a consistent cross-section, while stretch forming stretches metal sheets over a die to create complex shapes. Stamping involves pressing metal sheets into a die to create desired shapes, while deep drawing is used to create hollow shapes. Hydroforming uses fluid pressure to form metal into desired shapes, and other techniques include spinning, embossing, and bending.</p></p>
