It monitors network traffic, analyzes patterns, and alerts administrators when potential threats or abnormal activities are detected. On the other hand, cloud-based IDS relies on the cloud infrastructure to monitor network traffic and analyze data, offering flexibility and scalability.</p><p>The market for on-site and cloud-based intrusion detection systems is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.8% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing cyber threats, the emergence of advanced persistent threats (APTs), and the growing adoption of cloud-based services and solutions. Moreover, the use of AI and ML technologies allows IDS solutions to analyze vast amounts of data in real-time, enabling quick detection and response to potential threats.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the increasing adoption of managed security service providers (MSSPs) for IDS solutions. <p><p>On-Site Intrusion Detection System (IDS) refers to a security solution that is implemented within an organization's own infrastructure. This offers the advantage of greater control and customization but requires maintenance, updates, and investment in infrastructure.</p><p>Cloud-Based IDS, on the other hand, leverages cloud computing technology to provide intrusion detection capabilities. This eliminates the need for on-site infrastructure and allows for remote management and monitoring.</p><p>Both types of IDS aim to identify and respond to potential security threats, but the choice between on-site and cloud-based depends on an organization's specific requirements, resources, and preferences.</p></p>
